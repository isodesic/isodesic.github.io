Tiger Wall

A family of award-winning, ultralight backpacking tents with the highest space-to-weight ratio for a semi-freestanding architecture

Brand
Big Agnes
Category
Ultralight Backpacking Tents
Services
Product Design & Development
Release Date
Spring 2018

The Brief

Earlier in its history, Big Agnes created two of the most popular and iconic ultralight tent designs: the minimalist Fly Creek UL with its semi-freestanding single-hubbed pole and one front-entry door, and the full-featured Copper Spur UL with its fully freestanding dual-hubbed pole and two side-entry doors (except on the one-person size). The challenge, in the summer of 2016, was to create a hybrid that combines the best of both worlds: the lightest weight architecture that maximizes livable space and allows for two side-entry doors for ultimate ease of use.

The Process

Before exploring potential solutions, I surveyed the ultralight semi-freestanding backpacking tent landscape to understand how other brands were attempting to address this specific space. The closest competitors, which I viewed as the Nemo Hornet, the (older) MSR FreeLite, and The North Face Mica FL (which I was partially involved with), either weighed too much or didn't have enough usable internal volume. I then sketched out some potential architectures that allowed for more volume than the coffin-like Fly Creek UL design, but did not have as much pole structure as the more spacious Copper Spur UL design. Sketches transformed into a narrower set of more lifelike pipe cleaner models, of which a select set grew to full-size mockups in order to realistically evaluate livable space and measure the lengths (and therefor weights) of all the pole sections to see if I was on track with the weight savings I was targeting.

Three of the architectures were determined to be the most promising options, so I created initial sample requests for those three and shipped the pole sets to our partner in China for the patternmaking and sampling. One design stood out above the rest, and it was further refined over five more rounds of sampling into the final product that was released to the market.

The Design

The final Tiger Wall UL design ended up more similar to the Fly Creek UL architecture, but with pre-bent pole sections near the hub plus a wide ridge pole to maximize the volume in the middle of the tent so two people (in the 2-person size) could sit up at the same time and have plenty of room to change clothes. One key insight I discovered was that the specific location of the hub, high up on the tent, delivered much more usable volume while only increasing the weight of the pole set a small amount due to the extra sections needed on the two head-end legs. It ended up being a very worthwhile tradeoff. A door on each side, conveniently anchored from the tips of the ridge pole, allowed users to exit the tent independently. It was considered a “semi-freestanding” structure, which meant that while the configuration of the pole allowed it to stand up on its own, it needed to be staked out to maximize the volume and stability of the tent. 15-denier fabrics were chosen for the floor, body, and rainfly in order to delicately balance cost with weight.

The Outcome

The Tiger Wall UL2 and UL3 filled a gaping hole in the market and quickly took off. The product line was later broadened to cover two even lighter versions—Tiger Wall Platinum and Tiger Wall Carbon—as well as Europe-specific models with ripstop instead of mesh, mtnGLO models with internal LED lights, and Bikepack models that were optimized for bike touring. The Tiger Wall has won a handful of awards, including a 2018 Industry Award at the Outdoor Show (for the UL2), a Backpacker Magazine's 2019 Editor's Choice Award (for the 2 Platinum), and a GearLab Top Pick Award (for the UL2). I updated the Tiger Wall UL design for Spring '21 with a new solution dyed fabric (saving a huge amount of water during manufacturing), custom-designed TipLok pole & buckle attachments, patented Dirt Dagger UL stakes, and a large “3D bin pocket” for extra storage inside.
